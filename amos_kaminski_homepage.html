About

Amos Kaminski is a mathematician working on algebraic topology, algebraic geometry and category theory. His research investigates p-adic analytic Artin stacks, the derived Stone embedding and light profinite structures. In a recent preprint he resolved Clausen's Conjecture 5.17 by establishing a local light–profinite structure for smooth p-adic analytic Artin stacks through generalizations of van Dantzig's theorem and by constructing closed skeletons. Another work extends the classical Stone embedding using ∞-topoi to characterize profinite spaces in a derived setting.

Projects

Local structure of smooth p-adic analytic Artin stacks — Resolved Clausen's Conjecture 5.17 by proving that smooth p-adic analytic Artin stacks have a local light–profinite structure through generalizations of van Dantzig's theorem and by constructing closed skeletons. This result shows that such stacks are !-good.
arXiv:2603.00733
Derived Stone Embedding — Extended the classical Stone embedding using ∞-topoi to characterize the derived image of the pro-category of π-finite spaces in pyknotic spaces, thereby extending classical descriptions of profinite spaces to the derived setting.
arXiv:2410.24077
